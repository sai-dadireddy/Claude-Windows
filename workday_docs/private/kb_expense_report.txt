================================================================================
OVERVIEW
================================================================================

The Create Expense Report process allows employees to submit business expenses
for reimbursement. The process includes expense categorization, receipt
attachment, policy compliance checking, approval workflow, and payment
processing.

Business Process: Create Expense Report
Functional Area: Spend Management / Expense Management
Initiator: Employee (self-service)
Typical Duration: Same-day to 5 days (depending on approval chain)

Types of Expenses:
- Travel: Airfare, hotel, rental car, mileage
- Meals & Entertainment: Client dinners, team meals
- Office Supplies: Pens, paper, equipment
- Professional Development: Training, conferences, books
- Telecommunications: Mobile phone, internet
- Miscellaneous: Parking, tolls, tips

================================================================================
KEY COMPONENTS
================================================================================

1. EXPENSE REPORT HEADER
   - Report Name/Description
   - Business Purpose
   - Expense Period (dates of travel/expenses)
   - Currency
   - Total Amount

2. EXPENSE LINES
   - Expense Type (Airfare, Hotel, Meals, etc.)
   - Transaction Date
   - Merchant/Vendor
   - Amount
   - Currency (if foreign)
   - Receipt (attachment)
   - Itemization (for detailed breakdown)

3. COST ALLOCATION
   - Worktags (Cost Center, Project, Grant)
   - Percentage allocation (if split)
   - Billable to client (yes/no)

4. POLICY COMPLIANCE
   - Per diem limits
   - Receipt requirements
   - Approval thresholds
   - Alcohol restrictions
   - Prohibited expenses

5. APPROVAL & PAYMENT
   - Manager approval
   - Finance review (for high amounts)
   - Audit/compliance check
   - Payment processing (ACH to bank)

================================================================================
IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
================================================================================

STEP 1: CREATE EXPENSE REPORT
------------------------------
Navigation: Expenses → Create Expense Report

Employee Self-Service:
1. Click "Create Expense Report"
2. Or: From mobile app "Add Expense"

Required Fields:
- Report Name (e.g., "NYC Client Visit - Jan 2026")
- Business Purpose
- Expense Start Date
- Expense End Date

Optional:
- Memo/Notes
- Project (if project-related)
- Client (if billable)

STEP 2: ADD EXPENSE LINE - AIRFARE
-----------------------------------
Airfare Expense:

Fields:
- Expense Type: Airfare
- Transaction Date: Flight date
- Merchant: Airline name
- Amount: Ticket cost
- Currency: USD
- Receipt: Upload boarding pass or receipt

Additional Details:
- From City
- To City
- Round Trip or One-Way
- Class (Economy, Business, First)

Policy Rules:
- Economy class required for domestic
- Business class allowed for international >6 hours
- Must book 14+ days in advance (policy dependent)
- Airline fee reimbursable (bag, seat selection)

STEP 3: ADD EXPENSE LINE - HOTEL
---------------------------------
Hotel Expense:

Fields:
- Expense Type: Hotel/Lodging
- Transaction Date: Check-out date
- Merchant: Hotel name
- Amount: Total charges
- Currency
- Receipt: Upload hotel folio

Itemization:
- Nightly rate × Number of nights
- Taxes
- Resort fees
- Parking (separate expense or included)

Policy Rules:
- Max nightly rate varies by city tier
- Extended stay may require special approval
- Personal charges must be backed out
- Itemized receipt required (not just credit card slip)

STEP 4: ADD EXPENSE LINE - MEALS
---------------------------------
Meal Expense:

Fields:
- Expense Type: Breakfast, Lunch, or Dinner
- Transaction Date: Date of meal
- Merchant: Restaurant name
- Amount: Total bill including tip
- Currency
- Receipt: Upload receipt

Additional:
- Attendees: List names (for client meals)
- Business Purpose: Why meal was necessary

Policy Rules:
- Per diem limits by city (e.g., $25 breakfast, $50 dinner)
- Receipts required for meals >$25
- Alcohol may be excluded from reimbursement
- Client meals require attendee list
- Tip limited to 20%

STEP 5: ADD EXPENSE LINE - MILEAGE
-----------------------------------
Mileage Expense:

Fields:
- Expense Type: Mileage
- Transaction Date: Date of travel
- Merchant: "Personal Vehicle"
- Miles Driven: Total miles
- Rate: IRS standard rate ($0.70/mile for 2026)
- Amount: Auto-calculated (miles × rate)

Additional:
- From Location
- To Location
- Business Purpose

Policy Rules:
- Use IRS standard mileage rate
- Round trip distance
- No receipt required
- Cannot claim mileage + gas (double dipping)
- Commute miles not reimbursable

STEP 6: ADD EXPENSE LINE - OTHER
---------------------------------
Other Common Expenses:

Parking:
- Date, merchant, amount
- Receipt if >$25

Taxi/Uber/Lyft:
- Date, amount, from/to locations
- Receipt required

Tolls:
- Date, location, amount
- Receipt if available

Office Supplies:
- Itemize if multiple items
- Business purpose
- Receipt required

Conference Registration:
- Event name, dates
- Business justification
- Receipt/confirmation

STEP 7: ATTACH RECEIPTS
------------------------
Receipt Requirements:

Required Receipts For:
- Any single expense >$25
- Airfare (boarding pass)
- Hotel (itemized folio)
- Rental car (final receipt)
- All foreign expenses

Receipt Not Required For:
- Mileage
- Per diem meals
- Expenses <$25 (policy dependent)
- Parking <$25

Upload Methods:
1. Photo from mobile app
2. Scan and upload PDF
3. Email to Workday (receipts@)
4. Forward confirmation emails

Receipt Quality:
- Clear, legible
- All four corners visible
- Merchant name visible
- Date visible
- Total amount visible
- Payment method visible

STEP 8: ALLOCATE TO WORKTAGS
-----------------------------
Cost Allocation:

Required Worktags:
- Cost Center (always required)
- Project (if project expense)
- Grant (if grant-funded)
- Client (if billable)

Split Allocation:
- Single expense across multiple projects
- Example: Conference 50% Project A, 50% Project B
- Total must = 100%

Actions:
1. Click on expense line
2. Click "Edit Worktags"
3. Select Cost Center
4. Add Project/Grant if applicable
5. Set Billable flag if client expense
6. Click "OK"

STEP 9: ITEMIZE (If Needed)
----------------------------
Itemization Required For:

Hotel Folio:
- Room rate × nights
- Taxes
- Resort fees
- Parking
- Room service
- (Back out personal items like movies)

Conference Costs:
- Registration fee
- Materials/books
- Meals (if included)
- Workshops

Group Meals:
- Total bill
- Attendees split

Actions:
1. Click expense line
2. Click "Itemize"
3. Add itemization lines
4. Total must match expense amount

STEP 10: REVIEW POLICY COMPLIANCE
----------------------------------
Policy Checks:

System Validates:
✓ Receipts attached where required
✓ Expenses within per diem limits
✓ Approvals for over-limit expenses
✓ No duplicate expenses
✓ Worktags valid for company
✓ Business purpose provided
✓ Expenses within report date range

Warnings:
⚠ Expense exceeds policy limit
⚠ Missing receipt
⚠ Personal expense suspected
⚠ Duplicate merchant/date/amount

Errors (Blockers):
✗ No cost center assigned
✗ Invalid worktag combination
✗ Negative amount
✗ Future date expense

STEP 11: REVIEW & SUBMIT
-------------------------
Expense Report Summary:

Displays:
- Report name and purpose
- Total number of expenses
- Total amount
- Currency
- Expense breakdown by type
- Approval chain
- Policy exceptions (if any)

Pre-Submission Checklist:
□ All expenses added
□ Receipts attached
□ Worktags assigned
□ Itemizations complete
□ Business purpose clear
□ Personal expenses excluded
□ Policy compliance reviewed

Actions:
1. Click "Review"
2. Verify all details
3. Acknowledge policy compliance
4. Click "Submit"
5. System routes to approvers

STEP 12: APPROVAL WORKFLOW
---------------------------
Approval Chain:

Level 1: Manager Approval
- Reviews business necessity
- Validates expenses reasonable
- Checks for personal expenses
- Approves <$5K typically

Level 2: Finance Review (if >$5K or policy exception)
- Validates policy compliance
- Checks receipt quality
- Reviews cost allocations
- Verifies billable coding

Level 3: Controller (if >$25K)
- High-value expense review
- Special approval for exceptions

Each Approver Can:
- Approve
- Deny (send back with comments)
- Send Back for Corrections
- Add Approvers

STEP 13: PAYMENT PROCESSING
----------------------------
After Approval:

Automatic Actions:
- Expense report approved
- Payment queued
- Reimbursement amount calculated
- GL journal entries created
- Payment sent to payroll (or AP)

Payment Methods:
1. Payroll Integration (most common)
   - Added to next paycheck
   - Visible on pay stub
   - Taxed if appropriate

2. AP Payment (immediate)
   - ACH to bank account
   - Check mailed
   - Faster for urgent reimbursements

3. Corporate Card (if used)
   - Expense matched to card transaction
   - No reimbursement needed
   - Reconciliation automatic

Payment Timing:
- Payroll method: Next pay cycle (1-2 weeks)
- AP payment: 3-5 business days
- Check: 7-10 business days

STEP 14: POST-PAYMENT
----------------------
After Reimbursement:

Employee Receives:
- Payment confirmation email
- Amount paid
- Payment method
- GL posting details

Expense Report Status:
- Closed
- Archived
- Searchable for audit

Tax Implications:
- Most reimbursements non-taxable
- Exceptions: Moving expenses, excess per diem
- Taxable amounts reported on W-2

Use Cases for Automation:
- Bulk expense import from corporate card
- Auto-create reports from email receipts
- Mileage calculation from calendar
- Per diem auto-population for travel

Validation:
- All expenses have receipts (if required)
- Amounts positive
- Dates within report period
- Worktags assigned
- Policy compliance passed

Error Handling:
- "Missing receipt" → upload receipt

- "Invalid worktag" → select valid allocation
- "Duplicate expense" → verify not duplicate

================================================================================
BEST PRACTICES
================================================================================

1. Submit expenses within 30 days of transaction
2. Use mobile app to capture receipts immediately
3. Organize receipts by trip/project
4. Separate personal and business expenses
5. Itemize hotel folios to back out personal charges
6. List attendees for client meals
7. Explain unusual or high-value expenses
8. Use correct expense types for accurate reporting
9. Allocate project expenses correctly for client billing
10. Review corporate card statements promptly

================================================================================
NOTES
================================================================================

- Late expense submissions may be denied (60-90 day limit)
- Foreign currency expenses convert at transaction date rate
- Credit card rewards/points belong to employee (policy dependent)
- Missing receipts may result in denial or taxable income
- Personal expenses on business trips not reimbursable
- Alcohol may be excluded from reimbursement (policy dependent)
- Expenses must have clear business purpose
- Corporate card disputes handled separately from expense reports
- Audit may request additional documentation years later (save records)
- Expense patterns monitored for fraud detection
